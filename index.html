What is Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction?

Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) is an evidence-based program developed in 1979 by Dr. Jon Kabat-Zinn at the University of Massachusetts Medical Center. It teaches you practical skills to pay attention to the present moment — your breath, your body, your thoughts — without judgment. Rather than trying to "fix" or eliminate stress, MBSR helps you change your relationship with it.

The program combines guided meditation, gentle body awareness exercises, and simple yoga to help activate your body's natural relaxation response. Research has shown that regular mindfulness practice can reduce anxiety, improve sleep, ease chronic pain, and strengthen emotional resilience.

You don't need any prior experience with meditation. Whether you're dealing with everyday stress, recovering from illness, or managing a panic disorder, these techniques meet you where you are. Start with the breathing exercises below — even five minutes can make a difference.

Evidence-Based

Backed by decades of clinical research at leading medical institutions worldwide.

Reduces Anxiety

Activates your parasympathetic nervous system to calm the "fight or flight" response.

Improves Sleep

Quiets the racing mind and relaxes the body to support deeper, more restful sleep.

Breathing Exercises

If you're feeling anxious or experiencing a panic attack, these guided breathing exercises can help calm your nervous system. Find a comfortable position, and follow along.

Beginner

Box Breathing — Navy SEAL Technique

Used by Navy SEALs to stay calm under extreme pressure. Breathe in for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 4, hold for 4. This simple pattern activates your body's relaxation response and can be done anywhere, anytime.

5 Minutes

Quick Anxiety Reduction

A gentle 5-minute guided meditation combining a calming countdown with affirmations. Perfect when you need fast relief from anxious thoughts or the beginning of a panic episode.

Technique

4-7-8 Breathing Technique

Developed by Dr. Andrew Weil from ancient yogic breathing practices. Inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 7, exhale for 8. This extended exhale slows your heart rate and signals safety to your nervous system.

Quick Breathing Reference

Box Breathing In 4s → Hold 4s → Out 4s → Hold 4s
4-7-8 Breathing In 4s → Hold 7s → Out 8s
Extended Exhale In 4s → Out 6-8s (exhale longer than inhale)

Go Deeper with MBSR

Ready to explore mindfulness more fully? These resources range from guided meditations to complete MBSR courses — all free or freely accessible.

Foundation

Jon Kabat-Zinn on Mindfulness

Hear from the founder of MBSR himself. Dr. Kabat-Zinn explains the core principles of mindfulness and how paying attention to the present moment can transform your relationship with stress and suffering.

Practice

Guided Body Scan Meditation

The body scan is a cornerstone of MBSR practice. This guided session walks you through systematically noticing sensations throughout your body, releasing tension you may not even realize you're holding.

Movement

Mindful Yoga for Stress Relief

Gentle yoga is an essential part of the MBSR program. This accessible session combines slow movement with mindful awareness — no flexibility or experience required.
